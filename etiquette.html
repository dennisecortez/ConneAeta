ConneAeta | Nabuclod, Floridablanca, Pampanga

The Aeta Way: Protocols & Heritage

This portal serves as a cultural bridge, designed to foster deeper understanding and mutual respect. As you engage with the Nabuclod community, these essential guidelines reflect the values, traditions, and rights of the Aeta people, ensuring that every interaction is grounded in integrity, sensitivity, and shared humanity.

Cultural Etiquette: The Do's

Register First

Check in at the Barangay Hall to state your purpose and stay duration.

Ask for Consent

Seek permission before filming individuals, homes, or activities.

Bring Your Own Water

Highland water is scarce; ensure you are self-sufficient.

Dispose of Trash Properly

Practice 'Leave No Trace.' Bring down what you brought up.

Use Respectful Language

Acknowledge the Aeta people as equals and partners.

Coordinate Donations

Contact leaders first to provide what is actually needed.

Stay Mindful of Time

Plan visits during daylight for safety and resident privacy.

Wear Appropriate Attire

Modest, practical clothing respects the community's homes.

Greet the Elders

A simple nod or "Lapo pu" shows respect and builds rapport.

Educate Your Group

Ensure everyone reads the ConneAeta guidelines before arrival.

Verify Before Sharing

Ensure that any information or media you post online accurately reflects Aeta culture and prevents the spread of digital misinformation.

Check the Weather

Ask locals about trail conditions; rain makes paths dangerous.

The Don'ts

  • No Prohibited Items: No drugs or alcohol.
  • No Discrimination: No slurs (baluga, kulot, maitim).
  • Avoid Private Rituals: Respect sacred ceremonies.
  • No Improper Documentation: No photos for "clout."
  • Don't Misuse the Land: Avoid trampling on farms.
  • Be Mindful of Proximity: Respect local marriage customs.
  • Don't Ignore Advice: Follow the guidance of local officials.
  • No Excessive Noise: No Bluetooth speakers or shouting.
  • Don't Give Token Gifts: Avoid giving candy/coins to kids.
  • No Vandalism: No carving or painting on nature.
  • No "Souvenirs": Don't take wild plants or stones.
  • No False Promises: Mean what you say to the locals.
