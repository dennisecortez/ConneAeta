The Aeta of Nabuclod


The Aeta of Nabuclod are among the resilient Indigenous peoples of Pampanga, serving as the ancestral guardians of the mountains in Floridablanca. Their heritage is deeply rooted in a spiritual and physical connection to the land, which they view not just as property, but as a source of life, medicine, and identity.

For generations, they have maintained a profound knowledge of the forest—from traditional herbal healing practices to the rhythmic storytelling found in their music and dance. Today, the community continues to protect their cultural integrity and ancestral domains, inviting visitors to connect with their history through the lens of respect, dignity, and legal awareness.

About ConneAeta


ConneAeta is a digital platform designed to bridge the gap between visitors and the Aeta community through education and legal awareness.

By providing specific guidance on Aeta etiquette and integrating the principles of the Indigenous Peoples’ Rights Act (IPRA), the project aims to transform "good intentions" into purposeful support—ensuring every connection is rooted in mutual respect and cultural dignity.
