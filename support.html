Giving with Dignity

True support goes beyond hand-outs. It respects the autonomy and legal rights of the Aeta people. By understanding their rights and following ethical giving protocols, we ensure our presence strengthens rather than weakens the community fabric.

Ethical Giving & Support

Organized Giving

Go through the leaders

Don't give items directly to people you see on the road. Instead, bring your donations to the Barangay Hall or talk to the Chieftains. This way, the leaders can make sure the families who need help the most get their fair share.

Purposeful Essentials

Give what is needed

Sweets and toys are nice for a moment, but things like school supplies, footwear, or first-aid kits help much more in the long run. Before you pack, ask the local leaders: "What does the community actually need right now?"

Ethical Bounderies

Respect people's privacy

Helping others is a kind act, not a photo-op. Please don’t take photos of people receiving donations to post on social media unless they say it’s okay. Treat the community with the same privacy you would want in your own home.

Zero Waste Commitment

Take your trash home

The mountains don't have garbage trucks. Before you bring donations up, take them out of their plastic wrappers or cardboard boxes and leave the trash in the city.This keeps the Aeta's beautiful ancestral land clean and healthy.

Skill-based Support

Don't replace local skills

Try not to give things that the Aeta already make or grow themselves. The goal is to provide items they can’t easily get on the mountain, like specific medicines or specialized tools, so we don't accidentally discourage local work.

Digital Advocacy

Keep supporting from home

Your support shouldn't end when you leave. You can help by telling your friends about the Aeta's culture and their rights to their land. Being a "voice" for the community online is just as important as giving physical gifts.



Understanding The Indigenous Peoples' Rights Act of 1997

01

Ancestral Domains & Lands

This bundle recognizes the Indigenous Peoples' ownership of their lands, including the natural resources, waters, and spiritual sites within. It grants the community the right to stay, develop their resources, and regulate the entry of outsiders into their territory.

02

Self-Governance & Empowerment

The law respects the community’s right to decide their own priorities for development. upholds the validity of traditional leadership (Elders and Councils) and ensures their right to participate in decision-making at all levels of the national government.

03

Social Justice & Human Rights

This ensures that Indigenous Peoples enjoy the same basic human rights as all citizens. It provides specific protections against discrimination and displacement, ensuring they have equal access to employment, education, and basic social services.

04

Cultural Integrity

This right protects the community's "Soul"—their languages, religious rituals, traditional medicine, and artistic designs. It guarantees that their Indigenous Knowledge Systems and Practices (IKSP) are preserved and shielded from commercial exploitation without consent.
