ConneAeta | Advocacy & Integrity

Giving with Dignity

True support goes beyond hand-outs. It respects the autonomy and legal rights of the Aeta people. By understanding their rights and following ethical giving protocols, we ensure our presence strengthens rather than weakens the community fabric.

⚖️ Understanding IPRA Rights

Self-Delineation

The Aeta have the right to identify their own boundaries and manage their ancestral lands as recognized by the Indigenous Peoples' Rights Act (IPRA) of 1997.

Free & Prior Consent

No project, research, or large-scale activity should happen in Nabuclod without the Free, Prior, and Informed Consent (FPIC) of the community elders.

Cultural Integrity

Visitors must respect the right of the Aeta to maintain and protect their manifestations of culture, including archaeological and historical sites.

Privacy Rights

Just like any home, the community has the right to privacy. Respect "No Entry" signs or verbal boundaries set by the residents.

🤝 Ethical Support

Coordinate First

Avoid "drive-by" giving. Coordinate donations through the Barangay office or local Chieftains to ensure goods are distributed fairly.

Sustainability First

Support local livelihoods by purchasing Aeta-made crafts or hiring local guides. This empowers the economy more than one-time gifts.

Practical Needs

Ask what is actually needed. Often, educational supplies or health resources are more impactful than sweets or toys.

Long-term Respect

Think of your visit as the start of a relationship. True support is about shared humanity and long-term advocacy, not a single photo-op.
